Sarah Chen Appointed as Head of Global AI Ethics Council

Following her influential work in mitigating bias in machine learning models, Sarah Chen takes the helm at the newly formed international regulatory council.

Tech Daily • Oct 24, 2023 • 5 min read

In a landmark decision for the technology sector, the newly formed Global AI Ethics Council has announced the appointment of Sarah Chen as its inaugural Head. The council, established jointly by leading technology firms, academic institutions, and international policy makers, aims to create standardized frameworks for the ethical development and deployment of artificial intelligence.

Chen, previously the Director of Responsible AI at a major tech conglomerate, brings over fifteen years of experience in machine learning and data ethics to the role. Her pioneering research in algorithmic bias as fundamentally shifted how tech giants approach model training and evaluation.

“Our goal isn't to stifle innovation, but to ensure that as AI systems become deeply integrated into our daily lives, they serve humanity equitably and transparently.”

The Path Forward

The council's first mandate under Chen's leadership will be addressing the rapid proliferation of generative AI models. With growing concerns over misinformation, copyright infringement, and structural biases, the need for cohesive international guidelines has never been more urgent.

Over the next six months, Chen will lead a series of global summits bringing together stakeholders from diverse sectors to draft the 'AI Responsibility Charter'. This document is expected to serve as the baseline for national legislative efforts currently underway in the European Union and North America.

"We have a narrow window of opportunity to establish these guardrails," Chen noted during the press conference. "The technology is advancing at an unprecedented rate, and our ethical frameworks must evolve just as quickly."
